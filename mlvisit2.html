About this Event

Join 10 Media Lab students to learn more about their work and the work of their different research groups. After a short welcome, we will break out into small group conversations where participants can ask questions and share ideas.
Each conversation will be hosted by current MIT Media Lab students, who will talk about their research, the philosophy of their groups, and what goes on at the Lab in general. The event will be hosted on the Unhangout platform, a new way of conducting large, online conversations that was developed at the Media Lab. To prepare for the event, have a look at the How-To Unhangout instructions, https://unhangout.media.mit.edu/how-to-unhangout/ and make sure your computer fulfills the basic requirements. Sign up for reminders about the event and get ready to Unhangout!

Breakout Conversations

Changing Places

We propose that fundamentally new strategies must be found for creating the places where people live and work, and the mobility systems that connect these places, in order to meet the profound challenges of the future.

Lifelong Kindergarten

The group develop new technologies that, in the spirit of the blocks and fingerpaint of kindergarten, expand the range of what people can design, create, and learn.

Mediated Matter

Researches biologically inspired fabrication technologies. We research materials, fabrication tools, and computational design to produce novel products and architecture. We draw from biology, architecture, mechanical engineering, and computer science.

Molecular Machines

Focused on pioneering the field of Avogadro-scale engineering, which seeks to understand and approach the fundamental limit of engineered complexity.

Object-based Media

Make systems that explore how sensing, understanding, and new interface technologies can change everyday life, the ways in which we communicate with one another, storytelling, play, and entertainment.

Playful Systems

A new research group that explores how to design systems that become experiences by transcending mere utility and usability.

Responsive Environments

Explores how sensor networks augment and mediate human experience, interaction and perception.

Speech + Mobility

Creates technology to support groups, from families and friends to events and organizations. Our work explores multimodal communication, wearable technologies and mobile devices to connect people together.

Viral Spaces

Develops scalable systems that enhance how we learn from and experience real spaces, and builds new ways for media to directly interact with the spaces and objects around us.

Facilitators

Artem Dementyev

Savannah Niles
